## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines the standards of acceptable and prohibited conduct for all users of the Bazarix Marketplace, including Sellers who submit Listings, Buyers who purchase and download Digital Products, and any visitor who accesses the Platform.

This AUP is incorporated by reference into the Seller Agreement and Buyer Terms of Service and has the same binding effect as those agreements. Terms defined in the Seller Agreement and Buyer Terms of Service have the same meaning in this AUP unless otherwise specified.

ReadyPixels is committed to operating a curated, high-quality, and trustworthy marketplace for the Volqan framework ecosystem. This AUP establishes the baseline standards that make that commitment possible. Violations undermine the integrity of the marketplace, harm Buyers and legitimate Sellers, and may cause legal liability for all parties involved.

## 2. Prohibited Content — Listings

The following categories of content are **strictly prohibited** and will result in immediate Listing removal and may result in account suspension or permanent termination, regardless of the Seller's tier.

### 2.1 Malware, Backdoors, and Harmful Code

Sellers must not submit any Listing that contains, installs, or executes:

- (a) **Malware of any kind**, including viruses, ransomware, worms, trojans, rootkits, cryptominers, adware, or spyware, whether dormant at time of review or activated by a trigger condition.
- (b) **Backdoors or remote access tools** that enable unauthorized access to the user's system, server, database, or files — whether or not they are activated during normal use.
- (c) **Data exfiltration routines** that harvest or transmit user data, application data, environment variables, API keys, database credentials, or any other sensitive information to any endpoint not explicitly disclosed in the Listing's documentation.
- (d) **Logic bombs or time-triggered destructive payloads** that alter, destroy, encrypt, or otherwise harm system data under any condition.
- (e) **Cryptocurrency mining code** of any kind that consumes the host machine's compute resources without the user's explicit informed consent.
- (f) **Self-modifying or self-propagating code** that alters its own behavior after installation in ways not described in the documentation.

The presence of any such code — regardless of whether the Seller claims it is for "demonstration," "security testing," or any other purpose — constitutes a material violation of this AUP and the Seller Agreement.

### 2.2 Obfuscated Code with Undisclosed Network Calls

Sellers must not submit any Listing in which:

- (a) Source code has been obfuscated, minified beyond standard build tool output (e.g., Terser production builds), deliberately transformed to impede review, or otherwise obscured in a manner that prevents ReadyPixels from conducting a meaningful technical review.
- (b) The Listing makes network calls — including but not limited to HTTP/HTTPS requests, WebSocket connections, DNS lookups, and TCP connections to any external host — that are not fully and explicitly disclosed in the Listing's README documentation. Disclosure must include: (i) the full URL or domain of every external endpoint contacted; (ii) the category of data transmitted in each request; (iii) the purpose and triggering conditions of each call; and (iv) whether any data is persisted by the external endpoint.

Compiled output in `dist/` directories generated by standard TypeScript or JavaScript build tools is acceptable, provided the corresponding uncompiled source code is included in the submission. Build tool configuration (e.g., `tsconfig.json`, `esbuild.config.ts`) must be included so that the compilation can be reproduced and verified.

### 2.3 Intellectual Property Infringement

Sellers must not submit any Listing that:

- (a) Reproduces, adapts, distributes, or is substantially derived from any work protected by copyright, without the authorization of the copyright owner. This includes code, design assets, UI libraries, fonts, icons, images, documentation, or any other copyrightable element owned by a third party.
- (b) Uses any trademark, service mark, trade dress, or logo belonging to a third party in a manner likely to cause confusion, to suggest endorsement, or to dilute the mark, without the trademark owner's authorization.
- (c) Incorporates open-source components whose licenses are incompatible with the terms under which the Listing is distributed. For example, GPL-licensed code may not be incorporated into a Listing distributed under a proprietary commercial license without satisfying the GPL's requirements.
- (d) Plagiarizes, copies, or is substantially similar to any other Listing already published on the Platform by a different Seller, without that Seller's written authorization.
- (e) Uses another developer's or company's name, brand, or identity in a misleading way, including fake attribution of authorship.

Sellers who discover that another Listing on the Platform infringes their intellectual property should follow the DMCA procedure outlined in the Bazarix DMCA Policy.

### 2.4 Adult Content

The Bazarix Marketplace is a developer tool marketplace. The following content categories are prohibited in all Listings, screenshots, demos, README files, and any other materials associated with a Listing:

- (a) **Sexually explicit content**, including pornographic material, explicit nudity, or graphic sexual depictions.
- (b) **Content that is harmful to minors**, including any material that sexualizes individuals under the age of 18.
- (c) **Gratuitous violent content**, including graphic depictions of violence, gore, torture, or content designed to shock rather than to serve any educational or functional purpose.
- (d) **Content that promotes, glorifies, or facilitates harm to any individual or group**, including content that constitutes hate speech, harassment, or threats.

This prohibition applies to demo URLs, screenshot images, preview videos, and any linked external content submitted as part of a Listing.

### 2.5 Circumvention of Volqan Attribution License

The Volqan open-source framework is distributed under the Open Core Attribution License v1.0, which requires all deployments to display a "Powered by Volqan — volqan.link" notice unless the operator holds an active Support Plan subscription validated by the official licensing API at bazarix.link/api/v1/license.

Sellers must not submit any Listing that:

- (a) Is designed, in whole or in part, to remove, suppress, hide, alter, or disable the Volqan attribution footer component (`AttributionFooter`) or any equivalent attribution display mechanism, without a valid subscription license from ReadyPixels.
- (b) Replicates the functionality of the Volqan framework's attribution removal mechanism (the subscription-based license check) in a way that bypasses payment to ReadyPixels.
- (c) Provides tooling, configuration, or instructions that enable users to achieve attribution removal in violation of the Open Core Attribution License v1.0, regardless of whether the tool itself is labeled as an "attribution removal tool."
- (d) Intercepts, spoofs, or manipulates responses from the license validation API (`https://bazarix.link/api/v1/license`) to produce a false `attributionRemoved: true` response.

Listings that replicate core Volqan framework functionality for the explicit or apparent purpose of enabling users to avoid the attribution requirement without purchasing a valid subscription constitute a material breach of this AUP and the Seller Agreement and will be treated as an intentional infringement of ReadyPixels's proprietary licensing system.

## 3. Prohibited Conduct — Account and Platform Behavior

In addition to the content prohibitions in Section 2, all users are prohibited from engaging in the following conduct on or through the Platform:

### 3.1 Fraudulent and Deceptive Conduct

- (a) Creating multiple Seller or Buyer accounts to circumvent account restrictions, tier limits, or Platform policies.
- (b) Purchasing your own Listings (directly or through related parties) to artificially inflate sales counts or ratings.
- (c) Coordinating with others to submit fake positive or fake negative reviews.
- (d) Misrepresenting the functionality, compatibility, or quality of a Listing in its description, title, or marketing materials.
- (e) Claiming affiliation with, endorsement by, or official status from ReadyPixels, the Volqan project, or any other entity without authorization.

### 3.2 Unauthorized Access and Security Violations

- (a) Attempting to gain unauthorized access to any portion of the Platform, its infrastructure, databases, or administrative systems.
- (b) Using automated bots, scrapers, or other tools to collect data from the Platform in excess of what is reasonably necessary for permitted use, or in violation of any rate limits imposed by the Platform.
- (c) Interfering with or disrupting the Platform's servers, network, or other infrastructure.
- (d) Attempting to reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Platform's proprietary software.

### 3.3 Legal Violations

- (a) Using the Platform to distribute, sell, or facilitate the distribution of any content or software that violates applicable local, national, or international law.
- (b) Engaging in money laundering or financial fraud through the Platform's payment infrastructure.
- (c) Evading or circumventing economic sanctions imposed by the United States government (OFAC) or other applicable authorities.
- (d) Violating any third party's privacy rights, including by submitting Listings that collect or transmit user data without appropriate disclosure and consent.

### 3.4 Harassment and Abuse

- (a) Harassing, threatening, intimidating, or abusing any Buyer, Seller, Platform staff member, or any other person through any Platform communication channel.
- (b) Publishing or distributing defamatory content targeting any individual or entity through the Platform.
- (c) Contacting Buyers outside of the Platform in an unsolicited manner using contact information obtained through the Platform.

## 4. Consequences of Violations

ReadyPixels will take enforcement action against users who violate this AUP. The severity of enforcement action is proportionate to the nature, severity, and recurrence of the violation, at ReadyPixels's sole discretion.

### 4.1 Listing Removal

The affected Listing will be immediately removed from public availability. This applies to violations of Sections 2.1 through 2.5. The Seller will be notified of the removal with a stated reason.

Listing removal does not automatically affect the Seller's account standing. However, the Seller's tier eligibility may be reviewed following a removal.

### 4.2 Account Suspension (Temporary)

The Seller's or Buyer's account will be temporarily suspended, pending investigation, in cases involving:

- (a) Listings found to contain malware or backdoors (Section 2.1).
- (b) Suspected fraudulent activity (Section 3.1).
- (c) Suspected unauthorized access attempts (Section 3.2).
- (d) Second-strike IP infringement (as described in the DMCA Policy).

During suspension, the user cannot submit new Listings, make purchases, or access Seller dashboard features. Existing license keys remain valid for Buyers unless revoked for cause. The suspension period will not exceed thirty (30) days unless extended by an active investigation. At the end of the investigation, the account will be either restored or permanently banned.

### 4.3 Permanent Account Ban

An account will be permanently terminated — without the possibility of re-registration — in cases involving:

- (a) Submission of any Listing containing active malware, backdoors, or data exfiltration code (Section 2.1), where the violation is confirmed and not plausibly accidental.
- (b) Deliberate circumvention of the Volqan attribution license validation system (Section 2.5(b), (c), or (d)).
- (c) Third-strike IP infringement under the DMCA Policy's repeat infringer policy.
- (d) Fraud against Buyers or ReadyPixels of any kind.
- (e) Deliberate harassment or threats against Platform users or staff.
- (f) Any action that causes or threatens material legal harm to ReadyPixels, Buyers, or third parties.

A permanently banned account forfeits any pending unpaid earnings subject to the chargeback review period described in the Seller Agreement. ReadyPixels reserves the right to report confirmed violations of law to appropriate authorities.

### 4.4 Content Modification Requirement

For violations involving readily correctable non-compliance (such as missing disclosures, documentation deficiencies, or minor code issues), ReadyPixels may, at its discretion, permit the Seller to correct the Listing and resubmit for review rather than proceeding to removal. The Seller will be given a specific cure deadline that will not exceed thirty (30) days for non-urgent issues and forty-eight (48) hours for issues posing immediate security risk to Buyers.

## 5. Reporting Violations

### 5.1 How to Report

Any user — whether a Buyer, Seller, or general visitor — who observes or suspects a violation of this AUP is encouraged to report it to ReadyPixels. Reports should be submitted by email to:

**sharif@readypixels.com**

Subject line: `AUP Violation Report — [Listing Name or Issue Description]`

### 5.2 Information to Include

A useful violation report should include:

1. **Your contact information** (name and email address), so we may follow up with you. Anonymous reports are accepted, but we may not be able to update you on the outcome.
2. **The Listing name and URL** (or Seller username if the concern relates to account behavior).
3. **A clear description of the alleged violation**, including which provision of this AUP you believe has been violated.
4. **Any supporting evidence**, including screenshots, links, code excerpts, error logs, or other documentation that substantiates the report.

### 5.3 Processing of Reports

ReadyPixels will acknowledge receipt of violation reports within five (5) business days. Reports will be reviewed by ReadyPixels with reasonable diligence. Not all reports will result in enforcement action; ReadyPixels will determine in good faith whether a violation has occurred and what action, if any, is appropriate.

ReadyPixels will not share the identity of a reporter with the reported party without the reporter's consent, except as required by applicable law.

### 5.4 Good Faith Reports Required

ReadyPixels will not take enforcement action based on reports that it determines in good faith to be submitted for purposes of competitive harassment, retaliation, or other bad-faith motivations. Users who submit reports known by them to be false or misleading may face enforcement action themselves, including account suspension.

## 6. Appeal Process

### 6.1 Right to Appeal

Any Seller whose Listing has been removed or whose account has been suspended or terminated under this AUP may submit an appeal to ReadyPixels. Buyers whose accounts have been suspended or terminated may also appeal.

### 6.2 How to Submit an Appeal

Appeals must be submitted in writing by email to:

**sharif@readypixels.com**

Subject line: `AUP Enforcement Appeal — [Account Username or Listing Name]`

### 6.3 Information Required

The appeal must include:

1. The enforcement action taken against you (Listing removal, account suspension, or permanent termination) and the date it was communicated.
2. The stated reason for the enforcement action, as communicated by ReadyPixels.
3. A clear statement of why you believe the enforcement action was taken in error, including any relevant facts or context not previously known to ReadyPixels.
4. Any supporting documentation that supports your position.

### 6.4 Appeal Review

ReadyPixels will acknowledge receipt of your appeal within five (5) business days and will complete its review within twenty-one (21) business days of acknowledgment. If additional time is required for a complex investigation, ReadyPixels will notify you of the extension.

### 6.5 Possible Appeal Outcomes

Following review, ReadyPixels may:

- (a) **Uphold the original enforcement action** — the action stands as taken.
- (b) **Reverse the enforcement action** — the Listing is restored, the suspension is lifted, or the ban is rescinded, as applicable.
- (c) **Modify the enforcement action** — for example, replacing a permanent ban with a temporary suspension, or requiring corrective steps before reinstatement.

ReadyPixels's decision on an appeal is final with respect to the internal Platform process and is not subject to further internal review. Legal rights under applicable arbitration or court processes are not affected by the appeal outcome.

### 6.6 No Appeals for Permanent Bans Due to Malware

Accounts permanently terminated for confirmed submission of active malware, ransomware, backdoors, or data exfiltration code are not eligible for appeal. This exclusion exists because confirmed submission of harmful code constitutes an irreversible breach of trust that cannot be cured by corrective action.
