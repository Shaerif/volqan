## 3. Takedown Request (DMCA Notice of Alleged Infringement)

### 3.1 Who May Submit a Takedown Notice

Any person who is a copyright owner or an authorized agent acting on behalf of a copyright owner ("Complainant") may submit a DMCA takedown notice to the Designated Agent if they believe in good faith that content hosted on the Platform infringes their copyright.

### 3.2 Required Information

A valid DMCA takedown notice must contain **all** of the following elements, as required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3):

1. **Identification of the Copyrighted Work.** A description of the copyrighted work or works that you claim have been infringed. If multiple copyrighted works are covered by a single notice, you may provide a representative list, provided that each work is described with sufficient specificity to allow identification.

2. **Identification of the Infringing Material.** A description of the material that you claim is infringing and that you request be removed or disabled, with sufficient information to permit ReadyPixels to locate the material on the Platform. This must include the URL of the specific Listing page or any other location-identifying information, such as the Listing name and its Platform identifier.

3. **Your Contact Information.** Your full legal name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

4. **Good Faith Statement.** A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, the copyright owner's agent, or the law.

5. **Accuracy Statement.** A statement that the information in the notice is accurate, and **under penalty of perjury**, that you are the copyright owner or are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

6. **Signature.** A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or the person authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

### 3.3 Submission Method

DMCA takedown notices must be submitted by email to:

**sharif@readypixels.com**

Subject line: `DMCA Takedown Notice — [Listing Name or URL]`

Notices submitted by any other means may not be processed. Notices that do not include all required elements under Section 3.2 are defective and will not result in removal until corrected. ReadyPixels may, at its discretion, request clarification or supplemental information before acting on a defective notice.

### 3.4 Processing and Response

Upon receiving a complete and valid DMCA takedown notice, ReadyPixels will:

1. **Acknowledge Receipt.** Send a written acknowledgment to the Complainant's email address within five (5) business days of receipt.

2. **Evaluate the Notice.** Review the notice for completeness and apparent validity. ReadyPixels is not required to conduct a legal determination of copyright ownership or infringement at this stage.

3. **Notify the Seller.** Where technically feasible and where doing so would not delay urgent action required to prevent harm, ReadyPixels will notify the affected Seller of the takedown notice and its content, prior to or concurrent with removal.

4. **Remove or Disable.** Remove or disable access to the identified infringing material in an expeditious manner.

5. **Inform Both Parties.** Notify the Complainant of the action taken and inform the affected Seller of their right to submit a counter-notice under Section 4.

### 3.5 Effect of Takedown on Buyers

When a Listing is removed following a valid DMCA takedown notice, existing Buyers who purchased the Listing prior to removal will be notified of the removal by email. ReadyPixels will, at its discretion, issue refunds to affected Buyers if the takedown is upheld following any counter-notice process and it is confirmed that the Seller did not hold rights to distribute the content.

## 4. Counter-Notice Procedure

### 4.1 Right to Submit Counter-Notice

If you are a Seller whose Listing was removed as a result of a DMCA takedown notice and you believe the removal was in error — for example, because the material was misidentified, because you are the copyright owner, because the use is authorized, or because the use qualifies as fair use — you may submit a counter-notice to the Designated Agent pursuant to 17 U.S.C. § 512(g).

### 4.2 Required Information

A valid counter-notice must contain **all** of the following elements, as required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3):

1. **Identification of Removed Material.** A description of the material that was removed and the location at which it appeared on the Platform before it was removed (e.g., the Listing URL or Listing name and Platform ID).

2. **Statement Under Penalty of Perjury.** A statement, **under penalty of perjury**, that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification of the material to be removed or disabled.

3. **Consent to Jurisdiction.** A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which your address is located (if in the United States), or, if your address is outside the United States, any judicial district in which ReadyPixels may be found, and that you will accept service of process from the Complainant or the Complainant's agent.

4. **Your Contact Information.** Your full name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

5. **Signature.** Your physical or electronic signature.

### 4.3 Submission Method

Counter-notices must be submitted by email to:

**sharif@readypixels.com**

Subject line: `DMCA Counter-Notice — [Listing Name or Platform ID]`

### 4.4 Effect of Counter-Notice

Upon receiving a complete and valid counter-notice, ReadyPixels will:

1. **Acknowledge Receipt.** Send a written acknowledgment to the Seller within five (5) business days.

2. **Forward to Complainant.** Forward the counter-notice to the original Complainant and inform them that the removed material may be restored in ten (10) to fourteen (14) business days unless the Complainant notifies ReadyPixels that they have filed a court action seeking to restrain the Seller from engaging in the infringing activity.

3. **Restore Material (If No Court Action).** If ReadyPixels does not receive notice from the Complainant within the applicable statutory period that the Complainant has filed a court action, ReadyPixels will restore the removed material or re-enable access to it, in its reasonable discretion, in accordance with 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(2)(C).

4. **Notify Seller of Outcome.** Inform the Seller whether the material has been restored or whether a court action has been filed that prevents restoration.

ReadyPixels reserves the right to determine, in its sole discretion, whether to restore material following a counter-notice, and the submission of a counter-notice does not guarantee restoration.

## 5. Repeat Infringer Policy (Three Strikes)

### 5.1 Policy Statement

Consistent with the requirements of 17 U.S.C. § 512(i)(1)(A), ReadyPixels maintains and enforces a policy under which Seller accounts that are determined to be "repeat infringers" are terminated. ReadyPixels does not extend service to Sellers who repeatedly infringe the intellectual property rights of others.

### 5.2 Definition of Infringement Instance

For purposes of this Policy, an "infringement instance" is recorded against a Seller's account when:

- (a) A valid, complete DMCA takedown notice is received, and the Seller does not submit a timely and valid counter-notice, or the counter-notice process resolves in favor of the Complainant; or
- (b) A court of competent jurisdiction issues a finding or order that the Seller's Listing infringed the copyright of a third party.

Infringement instances resulting from takedowns that are reversed following a successful counter-notice will be removed from the Seller's record.

### 5.3 Three-Strike Enforcement

ReadyPixels applies the following graduated response to Sellers with recorded infringement instances:

**Strike One:**
- The infringing Listing is removed from the Platform.
- The Seller receives written notice of the infringement instance and a warning that a second instance will result in additional consequences.
- The Seller's tier eligibility is reviewed.

**Strike Two:**
- All of the Seller's Listings are temporarily suspended from public availability pending a compliance audit.
- The Seller's account is placed under "Restricted" status for a period of ninety (90) days.
- During the Restricted period, new Listing submissions require enhanced review.
- The Seller receives written notice that a third instance will result in permanent termination.

**Strike Three:**
- The Seller's account is **permanently terminated**.
- All of the Seller's active Listings are removed from the Platform.
- No future applications from the Seller (or from accounts that ReadyPixels reasonably determines are associated with the same individual or entity) will be approved.
- Pending unpaid balances will be processed as per Section 12.3 of the Seller Agreement (including a review period for chargebacks attributable to infringing content).

### 5.4 Discretionary Acceleration

ReadyPixels reserves the right to impose immediate permanent termination — bypassing the three-strike progression — where the severity of the infringement warrants it. Circumstances that may warrant accelerated action include, but are not limited to: submission of an entire Listing that is a verbatim or near-verbatim copy of a third-party product, systematic infringement across multiple Listings, or infringement conducted in knowing bad faith.

### 5.5 Record of Infringement Instances

ReadyPixels maintains a record of infringement instances associated with each Seller account. This record is retained for the period described in the Privacy Policy's data retention schedule and may be disclosed to law enforcement or legal counsel as required.

## 6. Good Faith Requirements

### 6.1 Requirement for Complainants

By submitting a DMCA takedown notice, the Complainant represents and warrants that the information in the notice is accurate and that they have a good faith belief that the complained-of use is not authorized by the copyright owner, the copyright owner's agent, or the law. Complainants are reminded that knowingly misrepresenting that material is infringing constitutes a violation of 17 U.S.C. § 512(f) and may subject the Complainant to liability for damages, including costs and attorneys' fees.

### 6.2 Requirement for Counter-Noticing Sellers

By submitting a counter-notice, the Seller represents and warrants, under penalty of perjury, that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification. A Seller who submits a false counter-notice knowing the material is infringing may be subject to liability for damages under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f) and may also face immediate termination of their account.

### 6.3 No Abuse of Process

ReadyPixels will not give effect to DMCA notices that are filed in bad faith, as competitive harassment tools, or that target content the Complainant knows or should know is not infringing. ReadyPixels reserves the right to reject notices that it determines in good faith to be abusive or filed in bad faith, and to terminate the accounts of users — including Buyers — who are determined to have submitted bad-faith notices.
